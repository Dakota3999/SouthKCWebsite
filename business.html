Business & Finance Institute

A Partnership between PREP-KC, Allen Village Charter School, Center School District, Hickman Mills C-1 School District, UMKC Bloch School of Management, State Street, and American Century Investments

What is the Business & Finance Institute?

The Business Finance Institute prepares urban high school students in the Kansas City region for postsecondary degree programs and careers in Business and Finance.

Engage during their 9th-11th grade year and continue through high school graduation.

Complete coursework with a concentration in business and finance

Participate in work related experience (i.e. job shadowing, client connected projects, internships, BFI summer experience) with PREP-KC’s business affiliates.

Develop career-readiness and employability skills rooted in business and finance.

Student/family commitment:

Students commit to completing all phases of the BFI program (including summer engagement).

Students commit to completing work related experiences (i.e. job shadowing, internships, mentoring sessions, client connected projects)

Students commit to remaining in good standings throughout their academic career.

There is no cost to the student/family to participate in this program.

BFI participants may earn and/or participate in the following Market Value Assets:

Complete dual-credit coursework related to business and finance, participate in client based projects and internships

Who can apply?

Any student with a strong interest in Business and Finance attending Allen Village Charter School, Center High School and Ruskin High School.

What are the requirements & suggested/not required prerequisites?

Students must demonstrate an interest in Business or Finance and be in good academic standing. Upon admittance, students must commit to participating in all phases of BFI through high school graduation.

In order to apply, a student should:

1. Complete the application:

PREP-KC Career Pathway Application (Allen Village Charter School)

SKC/PREP-KC Career Pathway Application (Center and Ruskin High School)

2. Submit one (1) teacher recommendation

3. Apply for the KC Scholars scholarship (9th grade)

4. Submit their transcripts, attendance and behavior report (collect from your school counselor)
